Foundations

Attune is grounded in contemporary neuroscience, cognitive science, and learning theory.

Its design reflects how biological systems regulate internal state through feedback, prediction, and adaptation, rather than suppression, reframing, or willpower.

Regulation is treated as a trainable capability, not a trait or diagnosis.

Core Concepts

Interoception

Interoception is the nervous systemâ€™s capacity to sense internal physiological signals, including respiration, heart rate, muscle tension, visceral sensation, and thermal state.

These signals form the raw inputs used to construct emotional and motivational states.

Improving interoceptive resolution increases the precision and timeliness of regulation.

Affect Labeling

Affect labeling is the process of applying a precise categorical label to an internal state.

Accurate labeling recruits prefrontal regulatory circuits and reduces ambiguity in state prediction. Precision matters: coarse labels preserve uncertainty; specific labels constrain it.

Allostasis

Allostasis refers to stability achieved through change.

Rather than returning to a fixed equilibrium, biological systems continuously predict demand and adjust internal parameters in advance. Regulation is therefore anticipatory, not reactive.

Predictive Coding

Predictive coding models the brain as an inference system.

Internal states are generated by top-down predictions and updated through bottom-up sensory error signals. Regulation improves when prediction errors are detected earlier and corrected with smaller adjustments.

Emotional Granularity

Emotional granularity describes the ability to distinguish between closely related affective states.

Higher granularity is associated with improved flexibility, decision-making, and resilience under stress.

Autonomic Regulation

Autonomic regulation refers to the modulation of sympathetic and parasympathetic activity.

Attune does not aim to suppress arousal, but to shift state efficiently and proportionally to context.

Habit Formation Through Cueing

Attune treats habit formation as a biological learning process, not a motivational one.

Rather than relying on reminders or discipline, Attune emphasizes:

  • intentionally provoked internal cues
  • repeated exposure under real conditions
  • closed-loop feedback at the moment of state change

Over time, regulatory responses become:

  • faster
  • lower-effort
  • more context-sensitive

Habits emerge as a byproduct of correct repetition, not as a goal pursued directly.

Levels of Engagement

Attune is designed to support multiple levels of engagement, each serving a distinct role in long-term regulation.

These levels are not hierarchical in value; they are complementary.

In-the-Moment Engagement

Brief, real-time interactions triggered by internal cues.

Purpose:

  • immediate state influence
  • early error correction
  • preservation of agency under load

Reflective Engagement

Short periods of review or consolidation after the fact.

Purpose:

  • reinforce learning
  • refine labeling accuracy
  • improve future detection

Structural Engagement

Occasional, deliberate training outside of reactive moments.

Purpose:

  • expand regulatory repertoire
  • increase baseline sensitivity
  • prepare for unfamiliar states

Each level contributes differently to the same outcome: improved adaptive regulation.

Design Position

Attune treats emotional and cognitive states as physiological signals governed by control systems, not narratives to be interpreted or problems to be eliminated.

The Attune Loop is the operational expression of these foundations.
